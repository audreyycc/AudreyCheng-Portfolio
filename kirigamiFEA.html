Kirigami FEA Project

Fall 2024

This project analyzes the out of plane deformation of a kirigami sheet for sheets of varying geometries and thicknesses. My team and I used Abaqus FEA to simulate a thin, neoHookean kirigami sheet with a fixed corner boundary condition. After using a buckling step to determine bucking modes, we applied a displacement to the top edge of the sheet and wrote a python script to run the simulation on sheets of different thicknesses and cut geometries. As a final project for ENG-SCI 128: Computational Solid and Structural Mechanics, this project allowed us to practice scripting in Python and computational analysis in Abaqus.
